The field guide

What we detect

Learn the enemy's playbook. Six of the most common traps — named, defined, and caught in the act.

№ 01✕

Ad Hominem

Attacking the speaker instead of the argument, so the point never has to be answered.

"You're broke, so your economic plan is worthless."

№ 02✕

Straw Man

Swapping a real claim for a weaker, distorted version — then knocking that one down.

"You want safer roads? So you want to ban all cars."

№ 03✕

False Dichotomy

Forcing two options where many exist. With us, or against us — no third path allowed.

"Either we cut every program, or the nation collapses."

№ 04✕

Slippery Slope

Claiming one small step triggers catastrophe — without showing why each link must follow.

"Let students retake one test and soon grades mean nothing."

№ 05✕

Appeal to Authority

Treating a claim as proven because someone important said it — never establishing why.

"A famous doctor endorsed it, so it must be safe."

№ 06✕

Hasty Generalization

Drawing a sweeping rule from a handful of cases — the exception dressed up as the law.

"Two bad experiences, so the whole industry is corrupt."
